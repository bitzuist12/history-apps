Based on The Infinity Machine by Sebastian Mallaby
Prologue
2009
London
Shane Legg warned that superintelligent AI could subjugate or annihilate humans. In the audience, Demis Hassabis gripped the armrest.

Chapter 01
The First Meeting
SpaceX — August 2015
The world's first AGI safety board. Musk hosted. Page, Hoffman, Hassabis attended. The meeting produced no agreements.
Musk used what he learned about DeepMind's progress to found OpenAI as a direct rival.
◆
Chapter 02
Project Mario
London — 2016
Every Sunday evening. Mint tea. No alcohol. Two North London boys planning to reshape the future of intelligence itself.
Hassabis's secret: 20 researchers training high-frequency trading algorithms. He wanted to beat Jim Simons. Google quietly shut it down.
◆
Chapter 03
The Bad Cop
London — November 2016
Google's chief legal officer arrived in London. "Everyone is in agreement on safety. But regarding the spin-out... there are concerns."
Pichai revealed the steelier side of his personality. AI was strategically important to Google. He would not let it go.
◆
Chapter 04
Plan B
Late 2016
Suleyman assembled lawyers, strategists, and bankers. The plan: $5 billion in outside pledges. If Google refused governance — they would walk out.
"It was open to Alphabet to just say: back in your boxes, boys, we own you."
— One of DeepMind's advisers
◆
Chapter 05
One Billion Dollars
Asilomar, California — January 2017
A serene seaside refuge on the Monterey Peninsula. Half a century earlier, it laid ground rules for genetic research. Now it was AI's turn.
Reid Hoffman committed $1 billion — more than a quarter of his wealth. 100x more than he pledged to OpenAI.
"I didn't tell Sam. I didn't tell Greylock. Billionaires answer to nobody."
— Reid Hoffman
◆
Chapter 06
The Vegetarian Curry
Silicon Valley — January 2017
What if DeepMind splits in two?
Pichai served a vegetarian curry and a tasty proposal. Perhaps hoping to drive a wedge between them.
Every few weeks, the 11-hour flight from London to San Francisco. Another meeting. Then back to re-scramble their body clocks.
That summer, the transformer paper appeared. Hassabis was less alert to its potential than he might have been.

Chapter 07
The Enchanted Forest
Scottish Highlands — June 2017
500 employees. Two chartered jets. A resort near the royal castle of Balmoral. The hall decked out like Narnia.

"DeepMind: A Global Interest Company." Two boxes. One dotted line. 500 people stared in silence.
Ten days later, Google sent back its position. Red lines all over it.

Chapter 08
The Mirror War
OpenAI — 2017–2018
"You are concerned that Demis could create an AGI dictatorship. So it is a bad idea to create a structure where you could become a dictator."
— Ilya Sutskever to Elon Musk
3:52 AM. "OpenAI is on a path of certain failure relative to Google. Tesla is the only path."
You're a jackass!
An intern asked if speed might be reckless. Musk stormed out. He never came back.
"Our biggest tool is the moral high ground. It doesn't matter who wins if everyone dies."
— Greg Brockman

Chapter 09
The Unraveling
2018
"An unprecedented technology requires an unprecedented structure." Putin: "The leader in AI will be ruler of the world."
Google signed a term sheet. $15 billion guaranteed over a decade. It seemed like victory.
Then Apple poached a senior executive. Jeff Dean was promoted. The space in the org chart — eliminated.
"He was up the cloud in a banana."
— A colleague on Suleyman
◆
Chapter 10
Coal Drops Yard
King's Cross, London — 2019
Hassabis liked to say the worst thing in the world was to control someone. But his hatred of domination ran the other way too: he was determined not to be dominated.
Two hours to decide. He paced through the rain. He called Marilyn — not a lawyer. He walked back in. He accepted the charges.
◆
Chapter 11
Placed on Leave
August 21, 2019
DeepMind's comms director was on a French beach. Her phone rang. Bloomberg would publish in one hour.
A message popped up on his screen. He put his face in his hands and walked out.
"Nobody said let him burn. There just wasn't a plan, and they were caught with their pants down."
Google made him a Vice President. He moved to California. A prince without a court. He was not allowed to manage others.
◆
Chapter 12
Idealist to Realist
2024–2025
Hassabis: CEO of Google DeepMind. Suleyman: CEO of Microsoft AI. Two North Londoners. Two tech giants. Bitter memories.
"You have to move from idealist to realist, but hopefully still with your values."
"Safety isn't about governance structures. Even if you have a board, it probably wouldn't do the right thing when it came to the crunch."
— Demis Hassabis
They had promised their technology
would never be used
for weapons or surveillance.

By 2025, both companies
were eager to supply AI
to the national security complex.

A flimsy scaffolding of reassurance
to an alarmed world.

But perhaps the best
comfort available.
