The inside story of how DeepMind's experiments in AI safety governance transformed Demis Hassabis from an idealist into a realist
Based on The Infinity Machine by Sebastian Mallaby
Chapter 01
The First Meeting
SpaceX Headquarters — August 2015
In the summer of 2015,
the most powerful minds in technology
gathered for a secret meeting
at SpaceX.
The world's first AGI safety board.
Musk. Page.
Hoffman. Hassabis.

All in one room.
The meeting produced
no agreements.
No conclusions.

Personal tensions overwhelmed
the discussion.
Worse, Musk proceeded to use what he'd learned about DeepMind's progress at the meeting to found OpenAI as a direct rival.
— The Infinity Machine
With that attempt at oversight stillborn,
Suleyman resolved to create
an alternative arrangement.

He imagined a novel,
post-capitalist form of governance.

Chapter 02
Sunday Evenings
A London Pub — 2016
Mint tea.
No alcohol.
Food ordered at the
last moment.

They talked into
the evening.
Most Sunday evenings,
Hassabis kept up the old tradition.

Two North London boys
planning to reshape
the future of intelligence itself.
Their codename for the talks
with Google:

Project Mario.
A 3-3-3 board: three DeepMind, three Alphabet, three independent.

Chapter 03
The Vegetarian Curry
Pichai's Home, Silicon Valley — January 2017
Pichai tried to break the deadlock.

At a one-on-one dinner at his home,
he served Suleyman
a vegetarian curry
and a tasty proposal.
What if DeepMind splits in two?
Hassabis could pursue AGI
as an independent spin-out.

Suleyman would run
all of Google's applied AI
from California.
Perhaps hoping to drive a wedge between them.

Chapter 04
One Billion Dollars
Asilomar Hotel, California — January 2017
If Google wouldn't give them
the governance they wanted,
they would walk out.
This is the most impactful technology of my lifetime. I'm in.
Showing considerable courage in his convictions, Hoffman agreed to commit more than a quarter of his wealth: an astonishing $1 billion. It was 100 times more than he had pledged to OpenAI.
— Reid Hoffman, at Asilomar
"I didn't tell Sam about this."

"I didn't tell Greylock."

Billionaires answer to nobody.

Chapter 05
The Enchanted Forest
Scottish Highlands — June 2017
500 employees.
Two chartered jets.
The Scottish Highlands.

The banquet hall was decked out
like the enchanted forest of Narnia.

Suleyman put up a slide.

"DeepMind: A Global Interest Company."

The room went silent.
500 people stared at an org chart
with two boxes
connected by a dotted line.
A spin-in AND a spin-out. People's heads were spinning.
Ten days later,
Google sent back its position.

Red lines all over it.

Pichai was nowhere near
approving the plan.

Chapter 06
You're a Jackass
OpenAI Headquarters — Early 2018
Meanwhile, at OpenAI,
the same war was playing out.

Musk wanted absolute control.
Altman was maneuvering
to push him out.
"You are concerned that Demis could create an AGI dictatorship. So it is a bad idea to create a structure where you could become a dictator if you chose to."
— Ilya Sutskever, email to Elon Musk, September 2017
Musk proposed that
OpenAI should spin into Tesla.

He was refused.
You're a jackass!
An intern had asked
if speed might be reckless
from a safety perspective.

Musk stormed out
of the meeting.
He never came back.

Chapter 07
Coal Drops Yard
King's Cross, London — 2019
Complaints were filed.
An investigation opened.
Hassabis recused himself.

The outside lawyer's report
ran to 25 pages.
Suleyman was given
two hours to decide.
He left the office
and paced furiously
through the rain.
He phoned Marilyn,
his girlfriend
from the time when his best friend
had been George Hassabis.

Demis's kid brother.
Later, he would wish he had called a lawyer instead.
He walked back in.

He accepted the charges.

Chapter 08
Placed on Leave
Mountain View — August 21, 2019
A message popped
up on his screen.

He put his face
in his hands
and walked out.
"Google DeepMind Co-Founder Placed on Leave from AI Lab."
— Bloomberg, August 21, 2019
He had thought that
accepting the complaints
would protect his reputation.

The headline shredded
that implicit contract.
"Nobody said let him burn, take him down. No one briefed against him. There just wasn't a plan, and they were caught with their pants down."
— A DeepMind colleague

Chapter 09
Idealist to Realist
2024 — 2025
The exercise had been futile.

The negotiations achieved nothing.
They were bound to achieve nothing.
They consumed vast quantities
of energy and goodwill.
"Safety isn't about governance structures. Even if you have a governance board, it probably wouldn't do the right thing when it came to the crunch."
— Demis Hassabis
"So then I thought, why don't I go the other way? Take the energy that was going into the trustless negotiation and put it into creating real trust."
— Demis Hassabis
Two North Londoners
with immigrant parents
now headed AI operations
at two American tech giants.

Although they were rivals,
with bitter memories of their parting,
they delivered similar verdicts.
"You have to move from idealist to realist, but hopefully still with your values."
— Demis Hassabis
The notion that a well-meaning individual
had a seat at the table
offered a flimsy scaffolding of reassurance
to an alarmed world.

But perhaps it was
the best comfort available.
