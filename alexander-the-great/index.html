Chapter I

The Boy from Macedonia

👶
Born in 356 BC
In the ancient kingdom of Macedonia, a baby boy was born who would change the world forever.
👑
Son of a King
His father was King Philip II, a powerful ruler who united all of Greece.
🌟 Fun Fact Alexander tamed a wild horse named Bucephalus when he was only 12 years old! Everyone else was afraid of it.
2
Alexander, age 12, bravely approaches the wild horse Bucephalus while others watch in amazement.
📖
Student of Aristotle
The greatest philosopher in the world, Aristotle, was his teacher! Alexander loved reading Homer's Iliad and slept with it under his pillow.
“
There is nothing impossible to him who will try.
- Alexander, as a young prince

Chapter III

A Dream of One World

💬
“Imagine all people living and working together — Greeks and Barbarians, side by side, as equals.”
Alexander the Great's vision for the world
🤝
Everyone is Equal
Alexander believed that all people — no matter where they came from — could live, learn, and work together. He was one of the first leaders in history to dream of a united world.
🏭
Alexandria: City of Wonder
He built a great city in Egypt called Alexandria. It had the biggest library in the world, where people from everywhere came to share knowledge!
6
Alexander's dream: Greeks, Persians, Egyptians, and Indians sharing ideas and friendship.
🤔 Did You Know?
★ Alexander wore Persian clothes to show respect for other cultures
★ He encouraged his soldiers to marry people from the lands they visited
★ He kept the local leaders in charge of their own cities
★ The Library of Alexandria held over 400,000 scrolls!
🌟 Think About It Alexander showed that being a great leader isn't just about winning — it's about bringing people together. What can you do to bring people together in your school or neighborhood?

💡

“
I am not afraid of an army of lions led by a sheep; I am afraid of an army of sheep led by a lion.

The world Alexander connected — from Greece to India, cultures blended and flourished.

The End

Alexander showed us that the world is small enough to explore and big enough to share. His story reminds us that bringing people together is the greatest adventure of all.
