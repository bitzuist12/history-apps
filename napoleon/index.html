Chapter I

The Boy Who Read Everything

👶
Born in Corsica, 1769
Napoleon was born on a small island called Corsica, just off the coast of France. He spoke Italian before he learned French!
🏫
Alone at Military School
At just 9 years old, he was sent to a military school in France. The other boys made fun of his accent and small size. So he spent all his time doing something else — reading.
📚
A Reading Machine!
Napoleon read hundreds and hundreds of books — about history, geography, mathematics, and great leaders. While other boys played, he sat by candlelight devouring every book he could find.
🌟 Fun Fact Napoleon was so small that his classmates called him "the little corporal" — but he didn't care. He knew that the real power was in his mind, not his fists!

Chapter II

Preparing Like No One Else

📚
“Before I set foot in Italy, I had read every single book about its mountains, rivers, and roads. I knew the land better than the people who lived there.”
Napoleon, on preparing for the Italian Campaign
🗺
He Ordered ALL the Books
Before invading Italy in 1796, Napoleon ordered every single book he could find about Italy — its topography, mountains, rivers, roads, and cities. He studied them all, cover to cover.
📈
Knowledge = Victory
Because he had read so much, he could surprise his enemies by taking routes through mountains they thought were impossible. His soldiers were amazed — it was like he had a magic map in his head!
🌟 Think About It Napoleon showed that studying hard can be more powerful than being the biggest or strongest. What could you learn about today that might help you tomorrow?

Chapter III

More Than a General

🏛️
He Created the Louvre Museum!
The famous Louvre Museum in Paris — the biggest art museum in the world — was Napoleon's idea! When he conquered Northern Italy, he brought back incredible paintings and sculptures from the Vatican and Italian churches to fill it.
🔬
A Scientist at Heart
Napoleon was elected to the Institut de France — the most important society of scientists and scholars in all of France. He was so proud of this honor that he wore their special uniform whenever he could!
⚖️
The Napoleonic Code
He wrote a whole new set of laws called the Napoleonic Code that said everyone should be treated equally — no matter if you were rich or poor. Many countries still use these laws today!
🌟 Fun Fact Some nights, Napoleon would sneak out of his palace in disguise with his secretary and walk through the streets of Paris. Their favorite stop? A little café where they would sit and drink hot chocolate like ordinary citizens!

Chapter IV

The Rise and the Fall

👑
Emperor of France
In 1804, Napoleon crowned himself Emperor. He was so bold that he took the crown from the Pope's hands and placed it on his own head!
⏳ Napoleon's Journey
1769 Born in Corsica
1796 Italian Campaign — his first great victory
1798 Expedition to Egypt with 167 scholars
1804 Crowned Emperor of France
1805 Victory at Austerlitz — his masterpiece
1812 The terrible march into Russia
1815 Waterloo — his final battle
1821 Dies on St. Helena, still reading books
❄️
Russia: Too Far, Too Cold
Napoleon's biggest mistake was marching into Russia in winter. The cold was so terrible that most of his army never came home. Even the greatest reader couldn't find a book to solve that problem.
8
Napoleon on the island of St. Helena — even in exile, he never stopped reading.
🏝️
Exile on a Tiny Island
After losing at Waterloo, Napoleon was sent to St. Helena — a tiny island in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean. He spent his final years there, reading and writing his memoirs.
📚

The End

Napoleon showed us that a small boy from a small island, armed with curiosity and books, can change the entire world. He wasn't just a general — he was a reader, a scientist, a lawmaker, and a dreamer who loved hot chocolate.
